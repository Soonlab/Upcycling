**Subject: Submission of an original research article —
"Comparative genomics of 111 livestock-waste MAGs nominates novel *Sphingobacterium* species with a vertically inherited *ure–cah* module for alkali-tolerant MICP"**

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript for consideration as an Original Research article in *Microbial Biotechnology*. The work applies an integrated comparative-genomic pipeline — spanning Bakta, Panaroo, GTDB-Tk, IQ-TREE, DRAM, dbCAN-HMMER, skani and mmseqs2 — to **111 metagenome-assembled genomes** (MAGs) recovered from four livestock-waste microbiomes (cattle, swine, sheep, poultry). We believe the manuscript aligns closely with the journal's mission to publish microbial research that couples mechanistic insight with biotechnological relevance.

**Main findings.** From the 111-MAG panel we identify a six-member set of MICP-complete MAGs distributed across **two functionally convergent lineages** — four *Sphingobacterium* and two *Pseudomonas*\_E MAGs — that retain the complete *ureABCDEFG* operon together with at least one carbonic-anhydrase gene. The operon is vertically inherited within each lineage: no transposase, integrase, prophage or relaxase is detected in a ±15 kb flanking window, and regional GC content matches the genomic mean. Rigorous permutation testing (10,000 iterations, Benjamini–Hochberg FDR) identifies nine trait modules significantly enriched in the hero lineages — most notably Mrp Na⁺/H⁺-antiporter complexes (10.9-fold), oxidative-defence enzymes (4.8-fold), glycoside hydrolases (4.7-fold) and carbohydrate-binding modules (9.8-fold), with bootstrap CIs excluding unity. Reference-grade HMMER dbCAN analysis corroborates the CAZyme signal independently. Principal coordinates analysis further reveals a **dual structuring axis**: pan-genome composition is primarily organised by taxonomy (PERMANOVA pseudo-F = 8.21, p = 0.001), whereas trait-module distribution is primarily organised by waste source (pseudo-F = 34.19, p = 0.001). Finally, two MAGs (S13 and S16) satisfy both GTDB-Tk and AAI criteria (< 95 %) for candidate novel *Sphingobacterium* species when benchmarked against the full RefSeq *Sphingobacterium* collection (63 reference genomes), nominating them as priority chassis for subsequent experimental validation of waste-coupled MICP.

**Significance for *Microbial Biotechnology*.** Field-grade MICP has relied almost exclusively on *Sporosarcina pasteurii* and a small set of *Bacillus*/*Lysinibacillus* strains whose growth optima are incompatible with real livestock-slurry conditions. Our work expands the MICP chassis palette to include two candidate novel *Sphingobacterium* species whose co-encoded trait stack (urease + CA + Mrp antiporter + oxidative defence + polysaccharide-degrading CAZymes) is mechanistically tailored to high-pH, polysaccharide-rich waste streams. The analysis deliberately uses non-canonical bioprospecting: rather than activity-first screening of culturable isolates, we mine directly environmental MAGs and statistically validate the candidate phenotype before committing to wet-lab effort. This strategy — and the open-source pipeline that implements it (https://github.com/Soonlab/Upcycling) — should be directly portable to other high-nitrogen waste streams worldwide.

**Scope disclosure.** This is an in-silico study. All genomic predictions are statistically supported, but biochemical and field validation (urease activity, CaCO₃ yield, sand-column UCS) are outside the present scope and are clearly framed as the next experimental step in the Discussion. We chose to submit the comparative-genomic work as a free-standing manuscript because its principal value is a prioritised, hypothesis-rich target list for the MICP community; we would welcome reviewer guidance on the extent to which such explicit framing is acceptable within *MB*.
